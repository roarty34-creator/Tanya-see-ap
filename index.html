Local Launch Guidance – Stilbaai Harbour
Use local harbour guidance, marks and current chartplotter data when leaving the harbour.

Harbour reference point in this build: -34.385542, 21.425433

Basic local route note
1. Move slowly out of the harbour. Stay near the middle of the river. Do not run too close to the western side because of shifting sandbanks.

2. Near the river mouth:
• Left (west): large shifting sandbank
• Right (east): rock / reef areas near the point
Keep slightly right of centre when exiting.

3. Approximate line used by many boats:
• -34.3869, 21.4226
• -34.3895, 21.4245
• -34.3920, 21.4255

4. Watch for:
• rocks on the eastern side
• sandbank on the western side
• breaking waves over shallow areas

5. Read the water:
• dark water = deeper channel
• light brown water = sandbank
• breaking water = shallow / rocks

6. First productive areas offshore:
• 30–35 m line
• Stilbaai Reef
• Kabeljou Ridge
• Blinde Rif

Tanya Deep Sea Garmin Edition • Weather
Check wind and swell before launch.

Good wind: under 15 km/h comfortable, 15–25 km/h workable, above 25 km/h rougher, above 30 km/h poor boat day.

Good swell: under 1.5 m comfortable, 1.5–2.5 m workable, above 2.5 m rough, above 3 m usually poor for bottom fishing.

If current is too strong, drift may be too fast for bottom fishing. Your line will angle away fast and sinkers may not hold bottom properly.
